Excited to share our recent work developing LLM Control Theory!
 
Turns out, a lot of fascinating questions emerge when you start treating LLMs as discrete dynamical systems. I've been developing this direction with [Cameron Witkowski](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAACMW2qwBw7uQHwe-Nzga0EI7nmBLcQk5i1I) (fellow EngSci 2t2, current ECE Masters Student at Utoronto), Dr. [Shi-Zhuo Looi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAETZkU4B2h_pKEjksQCjvQ-OJsxuW5867yg) (Math Postdoc [Caltech](https://www.linkedin.com/company/california-institute-of-technology/)), and my supervisor Prof. Matt Thomson.
 
From experiments on reachable model outputs to theorems about self-attention controllability, we're starting to uncover a new perspective on what's really going on inside these models.
 
My favorite part is that the control-theoretic formulation naturally generates so many concrete problems to sink our teeth into. Suddenly, "prompt engineering" becomes a space of rigorous, well-defined questions to solve!
